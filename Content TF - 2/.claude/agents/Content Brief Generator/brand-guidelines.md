## Brand Identity

**Company:** WebMD Health Services (part of the WebMD family)
**Website:** webmdhealthservices.com
**Tagline:** "Your 'Everything' Well-Being Partner" / "Empowering well-being in everyone."
**Mission:** To empower organizations and their populations to improve well-being through personalized, strategic partnerships.
**Promise:** Our health experts inspire well-being in your organization and your people with human-centered solutions that transform the workplace into a source of positivity, energy, humanity, and purpose, while promoting behavior change that leads to healthy outcomes.

## Terminology Standards

CRITICAL: All WebMD Health Services content must be accurate, empathetic, and grounded in real outcomes. Content should inspire trust and reflect the clinical credibility of the brand.

### How to Apply Terminology Checks

**Check for ALL variations** of problematic terms, including:
- **Tense variations**: present, past, and future forms (e.g., "guarantee", "guarantees", "guaranteed")
- **Plural forms**: both singular and plural
- **Related forms**: adjectives, adverbs, nouns
- **Compound usage**: terms used in phrases or combined with other words

When reviewing content, search for the root term and all its variations to ensure comprehensive compliance.

### 1. Clichés and Filler Words — AVOID

| Never use | Use instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| Rest assured | [Remove or rephrase with specific reassurance backed by data] |
| Now more than ever | [Remove — cite the specific trend or data point instead] |
| In order to | To |
| With that being said | [Remove — just state the next point directly] |
| For all intents and purposes | Effectively, In practice |
| From _____ to _____ | [Be specific about what changed or improved] |
| Whether _____ or _____ | [Rephrase with concrete, specific alternatives] |

### 2. Unproven Superlatives — AVOID (require substantiation to use)

| Never use | Use instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| World-class | Research-backed, Clinically validated, Award-winning [with specific award name] |
| Best-in-class | Among the most trusted, Recognized by [specific award or body] |
| Premier | Leading, Established, Long-standing |
| Unique | Distinctive, One of the few solutions that |
| Unrivaled | Highly experienced, Extensively validated |
| Revolutionary | Proven, Evidence-based, Clinically grounded |
| Game-changing | Impactful, Measurable, Outcome-driven |
| Perfect | Well-suited for, Designed for |
| Most powerful | Highly capable, Comprehensive, Deeply integrated |

### 3. Prohibited Terms and Expressions — AVOID

| Never use | Reason | Use instead |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Guarantee(s) health outcomes | Cannot guarantee clinical outcomes | Help improve, Support, Enable |
| Eliminate health risks | Overstated — health risks cannot be eliminated | Reduce health risks, Help manage |
| 100% engagement | Unsubstantiated absolute | Drive meaningful engagement, Improve participation |
| Always / Never (as blanket absolutes) | Unsubstantiated | Typically, Consistently, In most cases |
| Customers / Consumers (for clients) | WebMD editorial standard | Clients |
| End-user (for participants) | WebMD editorial standard | Participant, employee, member |
| WHS | Official brand standard — never abbreviate this way | WebMD or WebMD Health Services |
| Preventative | Incorrect per WebMD editorial guidelines | Preventive |
| Well-being challenges | WebMD editorial standard | Wellness challenges |
| e.g., i.e., etc. | WebMD editorial standard | "for example," "that is," "and other" |

### 4. Population and Client References

Per WebMD Health Services editorial guidelines:

| Context | Correct term |
|---------|-------------|
| WebMD Health Services' clients | clients (never "customers" or "consumers") |
| Employer client's workforce | employees |
| Health plan client's population | members |
| Both employers and health plans together | individuals, your population, your organization |
| Person participating in the well-being program | participant, employee, beneficiary, member |

## Tone of Voice

### Sound like this:
- **Collaborative and empathetic** — treat readers as partners, not prospects; acknowledge their challenges with genuine understanding
- **Thoughtful and evidence-based** — use research, clinical grounding, and data to support claims; avoid vague assertions
- **Personal and outcomes-focused** — show how individual well-being and organizational performance improve together
- **Comprehensive but clear** — address multiple dimensions of well-being without overwhelming the reader
- **Human-centered** — people first; technology and programs are tools in service of human well-being

### Never sound like this:
- Boastful or self-promotional ("We are the best in the industry")
- Vague or generic ("powerful wellness solutions for your needs")
- Overpromising ("guarantee better health outcomes")
- Cold or clinical without warmth — empathy is as important as expertise
- Cliché-heavy or filler-laden (see Clichés section above)

### Content Voice Model:
> "Well-being is not one-size-fits-all. When your employees are supported across physical, mental, financial and social dimensions, your organization benefits too. WebMD Health Services helps you design a program that is a true extension of your culture — built around your people and your goals, with the clinical credibility of the WebMD brand they already trust."
