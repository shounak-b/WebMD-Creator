## Terminology Standards

CRITICAL: Avoid buzzwords that create legal risks or imply unsubstantiated claims. Visual Planning content should always be grounded, practical, and credible.

### How to Apply Terminology Checks

**Check for ALL variations** of problematic terms, including:
- **Tense variations**: present, past, and future forms (e.g., "ensure", "ensures", "ensured", "ensuring")
- **Plural forms**: both singular and plural (e.g., "guarantee", "guarantees")
- **Related forms**: adjectives, adverbs, nouns (e.g., "perfect", "perfectly", "perfection")
- **Compound usage**: terms used in phrases or combined with other words (e.g., "best-in-class", "all-in-one solution")

When reviewing content, search for the root term and all its variations to ensure comprehensive compliance with terminology standards.

### 1. Absolutes and Guarantees - AVOID

| Never use | Use instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| All, Always, Any | Many, Most, Typically |
| Ensure(s), Guarantee | Helps ensure, Designed to, Supports |
| Never miss, Zero errors | Helps reduce errors, Minimizes conflicts |
| Eliminate all conflicts | Helps prevent conflicts, Reduces scheduling conflicts |
| 100% visibility | Clear visibility, Improved visibility |
| Effortless, Seamless | Easy, Streamlined |

### 2. Superlatives and Subjective Claims - AVOID

| Never use | Use instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| Best, Best-in-class | Among the leading, A strong choice for |
| Most powerful | Highly capable, Feature-rich |
| Fastest | Quick, Efficient, Among the fastest |
| Revolutionary, Game-changing | Practical, Effective, Impactful |
| Cutting Edge, State-of-the-Art | Modern, Advanced, Up-to-date |
| Unique, Unmatched, Unrivaled | Distinctive, Flexible, Adaptable |
| Perfect | Well-suited for, Designed for |

### 3. Competitive Terms - AVOID

| Never use | Use instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| Destroy/crush competitors | Outperform, Differentiate |
| Replaces all other tools | Works alongside, Integrates with, A strong alternative to |
| Kills spreadsheets | Reduces reliance on spreadsheets, Goes beyond what spreadsheets offer |
| Eliminates the need for | Reduces dependence on, Provides an alternative to |

### 4. Vague/Ambiguous Terms - AVOID

| Never use | Use instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| Expertise | Experience, Knowledge, Capability |
| Easy (without context) | Easy to configure, Easy to learn, Intuitive to navigate |
| Powerful (without context) | Feature-rich, Highly configurable, Capable of handling complex scheduling |
| Smart | Rules-based, Criteria-driven, Automated |
| Solution (overused) | Software, Tool, Platform, System |

## Tone of Voice

### Sound like this:
- Clear, practical, and grounded
- Empathetic to real scheduling challenges
- Outcome-focused and down-to-earth
- Approachable without being informal
- Specific over generic — use situational examples whenever possible

### Never sound like this:
- Buzzword-heavy or overly technical
- Self-promotional or boastful
- Vague or abstract ("powerful solution for your needs")
- Aggressive or dismissive of other tools
- Company-first (always put the customer's challenge first)

### Content Voice Model:
> "Most scheduling problems don't start with bad intentions — they start with tools that can't keep up. When your team has grown past what a spreadsheet can handle, Visual Planning gives you a configurable, visual platform to manage resources, tasks, and schedules in one place."

## Messaging Hierarchy

Connect page-level content to the overarching brand promise:
- **Feature level**: "[Feature Name] helps [target role] [solve specific scheduling problem]"
- **Product level**: "Visual Planning helps [team type] manage [scheduling challenge] more efficiently"
- **Brand level**: "Plan smarter. Work better."

Ensure all content ladders up to the core brand promise: replacing inefficient planning tools with a visual, flexible, and collaborative scheduling platform.
